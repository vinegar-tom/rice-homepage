about rice

Mark Engelmann and Toby Ramaswamy started a project called "rice" in early 2016, for a show curated by (now defunct) musician collective Six Families. Since then, rice has continued to compose and perform sporadically, in short bursts of intense work leading up to performances.

A foundational concept was to create a "rhythmic drone," where polyrhythms and harmonic motifs repeat until individual events fuse into a denser texture. Similarly important was a commitment to maximizing focus and comfort during performancesâ€”which led to an approach of composing rigid forms on a macro scale, while leaving room for improvisation within each moment.

rice's initial years focused heavily on keyboard and percussion instrumentation, generally with both players sharing both duties. In early 2020, James Blake joined rice to perform a live film score, adding processed vocals to the mix, with Mark and Toby moving toward more digital tools. rice continues to evolve as a trio, incorporating broader instrumentation and reexamining its core approach for each new performance.
